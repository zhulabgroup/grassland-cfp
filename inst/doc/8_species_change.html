Across the observations, species gained in communities by significantly increasing abundance over time (e.g., Bromus diandrus) or recruitment (appeared only after the 5th yr, e.g., Eschscholzia californica); in contrast, species lost from communities by significantly decreasing abundance over time (e.g., Festuca myuros) or extirpation (disappeared in the last 5 yr, e.g., Hypochaeris radicata). In the experiment, species did not experience complete turnover, but some gained by being more abundant (e.g., Brachypodium distachyon), while some lost by being less abundant (e.g., Torilis arvensis) in warming compared to ambient plots.
